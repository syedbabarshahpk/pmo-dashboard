How the health score is calculated
The health score starts at 100 and deductions are applied based on the following metrics. The final score reflects the overall delivery health of the project.
-2
per item
Delayed items (max -20 pts)
Work items that are past their sprint end date and not yet resolved. Each delayed item signals a missed commitment and carries over debt into future sprints.
-2
per item
Stale items (max -15 pts)
Active items with no update for 7+ days. Stale items indicate blocked work, forgotten tasks, or a team not keeping ADO current — all of which reduce forecast accuracy.
-1
per item
Unassigned items (max -10 pts)
Active items with no owner. Unowned items have no accountability and statistically never get done on time.
-20
pts
User story quality below 50%
If fewer than 50% of user stories have acceptance criteria, a 20-point deduction is applied. Without acceptance criteria, developers cannot objectively determine when a story is done, causing rework.
-10
pts
User story quality 50–74%
If 50–74% of stories have acceptance criteria, a 10-point deduction applies. Partial quality still signals process gaps.
-15
pts
ADO update rate below 50%
If fewer than 50% of active items were updated in the last 7 days, a 15-point deduction applies. ADO must reflect reality daily — Furqan cannot manage what isn't tracked.
-8
pts
ADO update rate 50–70%
An 8-point deduction for moderate update compliance. Target is 90%+ of active items updated weekly.
Score interpretation
70–100 · Good
50–69 · Needs attention
0–49 · Critical
